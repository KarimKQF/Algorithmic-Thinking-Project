Credit Risk Analysis Report

Algorithmic Thinking Project - Groupe NEOMA

1. Business Executive Summary

In the context of modern banking, minimizing default risk while maximizing loan approval efficiency is paramount. This project aims to develop a robust algorithmic solution to predict the probability of a borrower defaulting on a loan (loan_status).

By analyzing a dataset of 32,581 records, we identified key drivers of credit risk and developed three distinct Artificial Intelligence models:

  • XGBoost (Gradient Boosting) Top Performer: The top-performing model, offering high precision and interpretability.
  • Logistic Regression: A statistical baseline.
  • Deep Learning (MLP): A neural network approach for complex patterns.

2. Methodology & Data Analysis

2.1 Dataset Overview

The dataset contains 32,581 observations and 12 variables, meeting all criteria for robust statistical analysis.

  • Target: loan_status (0 = Repaid, 1 = Default).
  • Key Predictors: Interest Rate, Income, Loan Grade, Home Ownership.

3. Artificial Intelligence Models

3.1 Model Strategy

We implemented a Pipeline approach including:

  1. Imputation: Filling missing values (Median/Mode).
  2. Scaling: Standardizing numerical features for Neural Networks.
  3. Encoding: Converting categories (like loan_grade) into machine-readable numbers.

3.2 Model Comparison Results

We tested three models on a held-out test set (20% of data).

Model Accuracy ROC-AUC Recall (Class 1)
XGBoost 93.5% 0.94 High
Neural Network 91.2% 0.91 Medium
Logistic Regression 86.4% 0.78 Low

The ROC Curve Comparison clearly shows XGBoost (Blue Line) dominating the others, providing the best trade-off between True Positives and False Positives.

4. Business Recommendations

Based on our algorithmic findings, we recommend the following rules for the credit department:
  1. Automated Approval: Efficiently approve applicants with Grade A or B and a Debt-to-Income ratio < 20%.
  2. Manual Review: Application flagged as "Moderate Risk" (Probability 20-50%) should be reviewed manually.
  3. Risk Aversion: Monitor the Renters segment requesting large loans (> $15k), as identified as a high-risk cluster in our EDA.
